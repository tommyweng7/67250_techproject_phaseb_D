Enlivening Pittsburgh for Over 115 Years

Founded more than a century ago in the heart of Pittsburgh, this museum began as a showcase for Victorian-era ingenuity, featuring intricate automaton puppets powered by gears, springs, and early mechanical engineering. Visitors once gathered to watch lifelike robotic figures perform tiny theatrical scenes, marveling at the blend of craftsmanship and imagination. Over the decades, the museum evolved alongside the city’s industrial and technological growth, expanding its collection from clockwork marvels to early electrical experiments, video art, and computer-based installations. Today, it stands as a dynamic space where historic mechanical creations share the floor with immersive digital screens, interactive projections, and hands-on exhibits that invite visitors to explore how technology continues to shape artistic expression.

Our Timeline

1908 – The museum opens with 1800's technological art pieces.
1926 – Electrified kinetic sculptures and early electro-mechanical inventions are introduced.
1954 – Television experiments and video art installations enter the galleries.
1983 – The first computer-based digital art exhibition debuts.
2001 – Interactive projection mapping and motion-sensor exhibits transform visitor experiences.
2018 – AI, virtual reality, and generative art installations take center stage.
Today – Historic clockwork automata stand alongside immersive digital and interactive technologies.
